Effective Employee Referral Program

Nikhils profile photo

Nikhil Rawat

Published on LinkedIn
The recruitment efforts of an organization can be greatly aided by employee referrals or recommendations from the firms current employee about potential candidates. In fact, word of mouth recommendations are the way most job positions are filled. Managers have found that the quality of employee referred applicants are quit high because employees are generally hesitant to recommend individuals who might not perform well or fit into the companies culture. Generally, applicants who are referred by a current employee, if hired, tend to remain with the organization longer as well. A company's current employees may be the greatest source of future employees thanks to a well designed and well promoted employee referral program. Here are some suggestions for making your current program even better: 01. Make the employee referral program part of the company culture. Companies typically need a certain type of skills, education, values, and ethics in mind for their candidates. By educating employees of kinds of people the organization wants to hire and continuing to keep that profile in the forefront. Helping employees see how finding the right types of candidates will help enhance the overall team also adds to their enthusiasm for the program. 02. Be responsive. Failing to acknowledge referrals promptly makes employees feel as if their efforts have disappeared into a black hole, which is demotivating. Instead, let the candidate and referring employee know right away when a referral has entered the system. Then give the referred candidate priority processing in term of screening and interviewing to demonstrate how much good referrals are valued. Remember you are setting a precedence to the potential new employee on your referral program. 03. Up the Ante. Consider creating a two tier of financial incentives for referrals. Small reward for candidates who meet the company requirements and have been short listed, and a larger reward for a successful hire. 04. Provide rewards that employees value. Some companies offer healthy bonuses, donations to the referring employees favorite charity, or time off. However, sometimes the best recognition is a public recognition which can be equally rewarding. 05. Measure your results. Has to go without saying, that after the program has been implemented you need to study the results in term,s of the volume of referrals, qualified candidates, and success of new hires. These results can be used to find tune your program.
